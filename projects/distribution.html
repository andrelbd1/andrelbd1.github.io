A recommendation system to assign prosecutors in court

Full Report

As a Data Scientist at ECOA PUC-Rio in 2018, I had the privilege of leading a significant project aimed at enhancing the operational efficiency of the Public Prosecution of Rio de Janeiro.

The objective was to optimize the allocation of prosecutors to legal cases, by ensuring that each case was matched with the most suitable professional. To accomplish this, we embarked on a comprehensive, data-driven initiative grounded in historical legal data.

This project began by acquiring extensive historical lawsuit data, with a diverse array of metadata such as case type, cause, and origin. This dataset tracked the legal journey of lawsuits from the Court of Law from Rio de Janeiro to the Public Prosecution. One of the most time-consuming critical stages of the project was the data cleansing process, which ensured the accuracy and consistency necessary for meaningful analysis.

Through statistical methods, we uncovered important correlations within the dataset. These correlations were laid the foundation for building a decision tree model designed to automate the prosecutor allocation process. We selected this model specifically for its interpretability, which allowed us to clearly explain how recommendations were made and to foster trust among stakeholders.

We operationalized our model by deploying it as a RESTful API service, enabling seamless integration with the Public Prosecution’s internal systems. This allowed users to input key features of new cases and instantly receive recommendations for the most appropriate prosecutor. The outcome was a significantly more efficient allocation process that improved workflow, reduced assignment delays, and supported more consistent decision-making across the organization.

This project not only delivered practical results but also demonstrated how data science can be used to solve complex challenges in the legal domain, paving the way for smarter, fairer, and more efficient public services.
